My Top 10 Favorite Foods

Top 1

Fried Chicken

Fried Chicken is a crispy and juicy dish made by coating chicken pieces in seasoned flour or batter, then deep-frying until golden brown. It’s popular for its crunchy exterior and tender, flavorful meat inside.

 Top 2

Halo-Halo

Halo-Halo is a popular Filipino dessert made with crushed ice, sweetened fruits, jellies, leche flan, and evaporated milk, topped with purple yam (ube) and sometimes ice cream. Its name means "mix-mix," as it’s meant to be stirred together before eating.

 Top 3

Balut

Balut is a fertilized duck egg with a partially developed embryo, boiled and eaten with salt and vinegar. It’s known for its rich flavor and soft texture, often enjoyed as a street food delicacy.

 Top 4

Chicken Inasal

Chicken Inasal is a Filipino grilled chicken dish from Bacolod, marinated in a mixture of calamansi, vinegar and garlic. It’s known for its smoky flavor and is often served with rice and dipping sauces.

 Top 5

Bicol Express

Bicol Express is a spicy Filipino dish made with pork, coconut milk, shrimp paste, and chili peppers. It’s known for its rich, creamy texture and a balance of heat and savory flavors.
Top 6

Lechon Baboy

Lechon Baboy is a whole roasted pig cooked over an open flame or charcoal, resulting in crispy skin and tender, flavorful meat. It’s a staple at Filipino celebrations and fiestas.

 Top 7

Kutsinta

Kutsinta is a steamed rice cake made from glutinous rice flour, brown sugar, and lye water, giving it a chewy texture and amber color. It’s often topped with grated coconut and served as a snack or dessert.

 Top 8

Isaw

Isaw is a popular Filipino street food made from grilled chicken or pork intestines, marinated and skewered on sticks. It’s known for its smoky flavor and is often dipped in vinegar with chili and onions.

 Top 9

Arroz Caldo

Arroz Caldo is a Filipino rice porridge cooked with chicken, garlic, ginger, and broth, giving it a warm and comforting flavor. It’s often garnished with hard-boiled eggs, green onions, and fried garlic.

 Top 10

Kwek-Kwek

Kwek Kwek is a Filipino street food consisting of quail eggs coated in an orange batter and deep-fried until crispy. It’s typically served with a sweet and spicy vinegar-based dipping sauce.
